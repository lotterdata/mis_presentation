Real Goal: Understanding players' preferences

  • Players prefer some numbers, such as family birthdays, 7's, etc.
  • Direct data exist but are not public, so how can we document this?
  • Can back out by relating number of winners to numbers drawn.
  • Number of winners is only informative if total number of players is known.
  • Otherwise we can model parimutuel prizes as a function of the numbers drawn.

Modeling When Sales Data Are Available

Poisson Regression

Model TX1: \[ \ln(y) = \beta t + c +\ln(s)\]

where \(y\) is the number of 3-match winners, \(t\) is the sum of the numbers drawn, and \(s\) is sales.

Interpretation: \[\beta t + c\] estimates the log of the percentage of players who match 3 numbers.

Modeling When Sales Data Are Available

Modeling Prize Amounts

Without sales data, we resort to modeling prizes as a function of the numbers drawn. Linear models are not justifiable from an inference point of view, but they could provide good predictions.

Model PA1:
\[ y = \beta t + c\]
where \(y\) is the 3-match prize and \(t\) is the sum of the numbers drawn.

Some Refinements

Besides small numbers, players tend to choose combinations of numbers that are physically close on a playslip. So we'll add variables to proxy combinations selected along a row or column.

library(magrittr)

pa.train <- mutate(pa.train,range=n5-n1) %>%
            mutate(spacing = 
                  sqrt((((n2-n1)^2+(n3-n2)^2+(n4-n3)^2+(n5-n4)^2)/4)-
                               ((n5-n1)/4)^2))

Model PA2:
\[ y = \beta_1 t + \beta_2 r + \beta_3 s + c\]
where \(y\) is the 3-match prize, \(t\) is the sum of the numbers drawn, \(r\) is the range of the numbers drawn, and \(s\) is the standard deviation of the gaps between consecutive numbers drawn.

Interaction: Time Since Last Jackpot

Habitual players may have a stronger tendency to choose "favorite" numbers. So we calculate where each draw is in the sequence since the last jackpot.

draw.since.jp <- function(x) {
    jp.dates <- filter(pa.actual, prize_5 > 0 & drawdate < x) %>%
                arrange(desc(drawdate))
                    
    return(nrow(filter(pa.actual,
                       drawdate > jp.dates$drawdate[1] & 
                       drawdate <= x)))
}

pa.train <- filter(pa.train, drawdate > 
                       min(pa.actual$drawdate[pa.actual$prize_5>0]))

pa.train$draw.since.jp <- sapply(pa.train$drawdate,draw.since.jp)
